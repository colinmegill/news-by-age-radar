  • See the original data at Reuters

  • This graphic examines the primary source for news consumption of 5 age based cohorts. Older people watch TV, younger people are online. It's a smooth curve between the two, which visualizes the gradual generational shift towards new media.
  • Hover on a dot to see the exact percentage. Each ring represents 10%. The blue point on the top means that 60% of people 18-24 get their news primarily online. We can see that less than 30% of this group gets their news primarily from TV.
  • Saw this from @BenedictEvans this evening (6/21/2015). The original chart isn't *the worst thing they could have done*, they could have chosen 5 pie charts and wisely did not, but it was bad enough that I wanted to remap all of the dimensions. Noteably, they could have used stacked bar chart and mapped color + texture to media type and made age the x axis (what users will expect), which would have made comparison easier. The arrow on the top is also misleading and should not have been added, as the categories on the x axis are ordinal, not quantitative.
  • An interactive radar chart makes visual comparisons easier and was more fun, but the stacked bar is the more conservative choice.
