Primary Source for news consumption across age cohorts

See the original data at Reuters
  • Each line represents 10%, there are 6 lines, the blue point on the top means that 60% of people 18-24 get their news primarily online. We can see that less than 30% of this group gets their news primarily from TV (there's hope).
  • The original chart isn't *terrible*, they could have chosen 5 pie charts and wisely did not, but it's still worthy of scorn. Noteably, they could have used stacked bar chart and mapped color + texture to media type and made age the x axis (what users will expect), which would have made comparison easier. The arrow on the top is also misleading and should not have been added, as the categories on the x axis are ordinal, not quantitative.
  • An interactive radar chart makes visual comparisons easier and was more fun, but the stacked bar is the more conservative choice. Should be numbers on the axes but I did this in about an hour, so I called it a night. PR them if you like: https://github.com/colinmegill/news-by-age-radar. Someone already figured out the math: http://bl.ocks.org/nbremer/6506614. Also, the colors should match the original for easy comparison, but I used a simple D3 scale for that.
